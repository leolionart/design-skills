# Anti Design

## Thesis
Break alignment, rhythm, and safe defaults on purpose so the page feels human, opinionated, and resistant to polished template sameness.

## Best when
- the brief says anti-design, deconstructed, intentional disorder, raw, offbeat, or anti-template
- culture, art, fashion, and unconventional brands want visible tension over smoothness
- the page can trade some usability comfort for stronger identity
- motion tolerance is low to medium

## Distinguishing signals
- rawer and less structured than `neo-brutalism`
- less publication-led than `brutalist-editorial-zine`
- less glossy and nostalgic than `y2k-retro-futurism`
- less friendly than `cute-alism-kawaii-brutalism`

## Layout directives
- use broken rhythm and deliberate interruption around one recoverable reading spine
- let sections feel visibly authored, not auto-generated
- keep enough structure that the user can still navigate the page
- use tension through spacing, overlap, and misalignment intentionally

## Typography and surface directives
- typography can feel raw, offset, or intentionally awkward
- surfaces should support the anti-template thesis rather than look polished by default
- color and border aggression should feel chosen, not random
- keep one anchor of order so disruption reads as authorship

## Component behavior
- CTA can feel unconventional but must stay findable
- modules may interrupt each other if priority remains recoverable
- nav should not become so broken that orientation is lost
- proof should feel contextual and authored rather than startup-generic

## Avoid
- random mess with no thesis
- every element fighting equally for attention
- disruption that destroys task flow completely
- glossy or nostalgic styling that belongs to `y2k-retro-futurism`

## Review and conversion cues
- If the page feels publication-like with stronger text-image logic, it is drifting toward `brutalist-editorial-zine`.
- If the page feels hard-edged but still highly structured, it is drifting toward `neo-brutalism`.
- To convert from safer bold pages, break one default at a time and keep one hierarchy spine visible.
