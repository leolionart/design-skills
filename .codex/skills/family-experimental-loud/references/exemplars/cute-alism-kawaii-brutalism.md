# Cute-alism Kawaii Brutalism

## Thesis
Combine bold geometry and high-contrast structure with cheerful, character-led, or sticker-like friendliness so the interface feels punchy and playful at the same time.

## Best when
- the brief says cute, mascot-led, playful, youth-oriented, sticker-like, cheerful, or family-friendly but still bold
- consumer apps, creator tools, youth culture, or playful product launches
- stronger personality is needed without going full chaotic experimental
- low-to-medium motion can be playful

## Distinguishing signals
- friendlier and more character-led than `neo-brutalism`
- punchier and more contrast-heavy than `tactile-organic`
- less raw than `anti-design`
- less nostalgic and metallic than `y2k-retro-futurism`

## Layout directives
- keep the structure bold and easy to parse
- use playful interruptions, stickers, mascots, or accent shapes around a clear hierarchy
- let the page feel energetic, but preserve one obvious reading order
- use chunked sections rather than editorial collage

## Typography and surface directives
- use confident, rounded, or cheerful type with enough weight
- colors can be vivid, but should stay authored rather than random
- surfaces can be simple and punchy; avoid over-softening everything
- icons or mascots should support emphasis points, not flood the page

## Component behavior
- CTA can feel lively and expressive
- cards can use stronger outlines, fun labels, or sticker-like accents
- nav should stay readable and less ornamental than hero elements
- onboarding or benefit sections can use playful modularity

## Avoid
- softening the whole page until it becomes `tactile-organic`
- turning playfulness into childish clutter
- letting every decorative character compete for attention
- using brutal contrast without any warmth

## Review and conversion cues
- If the page feels mostly soft and ambient, it is drifting toward `tactile-organic`.
- If the page feels hard-edged but not friendly, it is drifting toward `neo-brutalism`.
- To convert from `neo-brutalism`, preserve structure but add warmth, mascots, and playful emphasis rather than raw force.
