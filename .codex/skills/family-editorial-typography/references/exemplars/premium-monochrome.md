# Premium Monochrome

## Thesis
Use neutral color, fine borders, and spacious typographic precision to create a quiet luxury interface where restraint itself feels expensive.

## Best when
- the brief says quiet luxury, boutique, expensive, monochrome, or refined founder brand
- fashion, architecture, boutique services, or luxury commerce need authority without noise
- the page should feel polished, restrained, and premium-neutral
- motion tolerance is low

## Distinguishing signals
- colder and more neutral than `meaningful-minimalism`
- more luxurious and less editorial-warm than `editorial-minimalism`
- less typographically forceful than `typography-first`
- depends on tone, spacing, and finish rather than expressive type or color

## Layout directives
- keep the page highly ordered and spacious
- use symmetry or near-symmetry more comfortably than other editorial variants
- let each section feel resolved and polished rather than dynamic
- keep hierarchy clear through alignment and spacing, not visual tricks

## Typography and surface directives
- use serif-sans pairing with strict restraint
- keep palette near monochrome or neutral with very selective accent use
- favor fine borders, subtle surfaces, and controlled shadow
- let spacing discipline carry the premium feel

## Component behavior
- CTA should feel precise and calm, never loud
- cards and supporting modules should feel expensive through finish, not novelty
- proof should be sparse and curated
- imagery should feel art-directed and polished, not casual

## Avoid
- warmth or softness that turns it into `meaningful-minimalism`
- too much editorial roughness or visible publication tension
- decorative gradients or playful color
- dense product explanation that weakens the luxury tone

## Review and conversion cues
- If the page feels calm, humane, and softer than expensive, it is drifting toward `meaningful-minimalism`.
- If it feels more like a manifesto or publication, it is drifting toward `editorial-minimalism`.
- To convert from richer or louder premium pages, reduce color first, then tighten spacing and surface restraint.
