# Dark Glow Aurora

## Thesis
Use a dark canvas, luminous fields, and controlled aurora atmosphere to make the interface feel cinematic, future-facing, and premium-tech without losing hierarchy.

## Best when
- the brief says dark premium tech, aurora, cinematic, glow, devtools, AI launch, or future-facing
- the hero needs strong mood and atmosphere
- the page can trade some neutrality for premium drama
- motion tolerance is medium

## Distinguishing signals
- darker and more atmospheric than `glassmorphism-mature`
- less object-staged than `immersive-3d-product`
- less reveal-structured than `motion-led-storytelling`
- less reflective and fashion-coded than `iridescent-holographic-chrome`

## Layout directives
- stage one strong dark hero with clear luminous focus
- keep supporting sections calmer than the hero so atmosphere stays special
- use depth and glow to frame priority, not to flatten everything into ambience
- let narrative pacing stay simple unless the brief strongly asks for scrollytelling

## Typography and surface directives
- keep headlines dramatic but highly legible
- use glow and aurora fields selectively around key focus zones
- keep surfaces dark but differentiated enough for reading and CTA clarity
- use accent color with discipline so the page stays premium instead of noisy

## Component behavior
- hero should carry the atmospheric load
- CTA must remain readable against dark surfaces
- cards and proof panels should feel layered, not neon-decorated
- supporting modules should restore clarity after the hero spectacle

## Avoid
- glow covering the whole UI
- long-form reading on overly dark surfaces without contrast control
- reflective novelty that should become `iridescent-holographic-chrome`
- 3D spectacle with no structural role

## Review and conversion cues
- If the page feels readable through layered translucent UI rather than dark mood, it is drifting toward `glassmorphism-mature`.
- If the hero is really about one product object, it is drifting toward `immersive-3d-product`.
- To convert from lighter premium pages, darken the canvas first, then add luminous focal fields selectively.
