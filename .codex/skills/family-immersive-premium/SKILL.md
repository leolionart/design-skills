# Family Immersive Premium

Use when the UI should feel layered, luminous, cinematic, or premium-tech while still preserving hierarchy and readability.

## Handle these jobs
- design premium launches with atmosphere
- review glass, glow, aurora, or 3D-heavy pages
- turn flat marketing into layered premium storytelling
- convert product-safe layouts into more immersive hero-led experiences

## Family thesis
Use depth, light, and staging to create atmosphere. Let spectacle support hierarchy instead of replacing it.

## Use this family when
- the brief mentions premium tech, cinematic, glass, glow, 3D, layered, or immersive
- the hero needs strong mood and focal depth
- narrative reveal matters more than pure scan speed

## Avoid this family when
- the page is dense, operational, or table-heavy
- the team needs maximum neutrality and directness
- glow, blur, or motion would damage clarity

## Always cover
- focal depth and hero staging
- atmosphere vs readability balance
- surface and material language
- motion budget
- what effects must be reduced or removed

## Output contract
Return:
1. family thesis
2. hierarchy and staging rules
3. surface and motion treatment
4. component directives
5. avoid list
6. review or conversion moves

## Guardrails
- Name one focal scene before adding effects.
- Keep text contrast and reading rhythm explicit.
- Use glow, blur, or 3D only when they clarify the premium story.
- Remove fake-premium decoration with no structural role.
