# Family Tactile Organic

Use when the interface should feel warm, soft, material, and approachable rather than sharp, formal, or aggressively technical.

## Handle these jobs
- design softer onboarding and consumer flows
- review whether rounded and tactile cues are coherent
- convert hard-edged layouts into warmer product directions
- make ambient gradients or soft depth feel useful rather than childish

## Family thesis
Create friendliness through rounded geometry, gentle surfaces, and soft visual transitions while keeping enough contrast and structure to stay usable.

## Use this family when
- the brief mentions soft, friendly, tactile, rounded, playful, wellness, or approachable
- the product benefits from warmth and emotional ease
- the page should feel inviting instead of forceful

## Avoid this family when
- the interface is enterprise-heavy, data-dense, or precision-driven
- the brand needs sharp tension or luxury restraint
- softness would undermine trust or clarity

## Always cover
- rounded geometry and material feel
- warmth vs childishness balance
- contrast discipline
- ambient color or surface guidance
- what excess softness to remove

## Output contract
Return:
1. family thesis
2. layout and geometry language
3. surface and color treatment
4. component directives
5. avoid list
6. review or conversion moves

## Guardrails
- Keep softness structured.
- Do not let blobs, depth, or color clouds erase hierarchy.
- Preserve accessible contrast.
- Use playfulness as warmth, not as noise.
