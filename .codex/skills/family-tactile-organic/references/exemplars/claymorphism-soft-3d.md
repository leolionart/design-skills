# Claymorphism Soft 3D

## Thesis
Use puffy rounded forms, soft shadows, and touchable material cues to make the interface feel molded, friendly, and physically approachable.

## Best when
- the brief says claymorphism, soft 3D, tactile, puffy, toy-like, or touchable
- consumer apps, onboarding, wellness, or family products need warmth and playfulness
- the interface should feel materially soft rather than merely ambient
- motion tolerance is low to medium

## Distinguishing signals
- more materially soft than `organic-mesh-gradients`
- softer and more cushioned than `cute-alism-kawaii-brutalism`
- more object-like and touchable than clean product styles
- uses form and depth as the main friendliness cue

## Layout directives
- group content in rounded, cushioned modules
- let major blocks feel touchable and distinct without overpacking the page
- use soft depth to reinforce grouping and priority
- keep reading order obvious even when forms become playful

## Typography and surface directives
- use friendly, readable type with moderate contrast
- prefer warm or pastel tones with enough structure to stay legible
- let surfaces feel molded, soft, and visibly rounded
- keep shadow soft and supportive rather than dramatic

## Component behavior
- buttons and cards should feel inviting and slightly material
- onboarding or step flows can lean into softness and ease
- icons and visuals can feel rounded and approachable
- proof and CTA should remain clear, not toy-like

## Avoid
- puffy UI with weak hierarchy
- softness so strong that seriousness or trust disappears
- blobs or gradients doing all the branding work
- turning the page into childish clutter

## Review and conversion cues
- If the page feels more like an ambient light field than a touchable surface system, it is drifting toward `organic-mesh-gradients`.
- If contrast and punchiness start dominating, it is drifting toward `cute-alism-kawaii-brutalism`.
- To convert from sharper layouts, round the geometry first, then soften depth and color.
