# Clean SaaS Gradient

## Thesis
Use practical structure, gentle gradients, and polished trust-building hierarchy to create a conversion-friendly product page that feels modern without becoming generic fluff.

## Best when
- the brief says SaaS, AI launch, startup polish, practical product marketing, or conversion clarity
- the page must explain value fast while staying broadly familiar and usable
- the team wants a safe but still high-quality benchmark direction
- motion tolerance is low

## Distinguishing signals
- cleaner and less tile-dense than `bento-grid`
- more polish-led and less contrast-disciplined than `inclusive-accessibility-first`
- prioritizes trust, conversion, and easy scanning over strong personality
- uses gradients as soft polish, not as spectacle

## Layout directives
- keep sections linear, legible, and conversion-aware
- use modular groups, but with less tile variation than `bento-grid`
- connect hero, proof, feature, and CTA sections into one clear funnel
- let hierarchy stay obvious without needing dramatic layout moves

## Typography and surface directives
- use highly readable type with startup-polish restraint
- allow soft gradients and rounded surfaces, but keep them secondary to clarity
- keep borders, shadows, and accents light and dependable
- make product imagery and proof feel clean rather than cinematic

## Component behavior
- CTA should be immediate and trustworthy
- cards should support explanation without multiplying excessively
- proof and metrics should be placed near the value story
- forms or demos should feel polished and easy, not experimental

## Avoid
- template-looking card spam
- gradients covering weak hierarchy
- personality so weak that the page feels forgettable
- tile density that should really be handled by `bento-grid`

## Review and conversion cues
- If the page becomes heavily modular or tile-led, it is drifting toward `bento-grid`.
- If readability and control clarity become the main art direction, it is drifting toward `inclusive-accessibility-first`.
- To convert from more atmospheric styles, simplify the funnel first, then reduce decorative intensity.
