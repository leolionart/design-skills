# Default High Agency

## Thesis
Use asymmetry, decisive hierarchy, and changing section rhythm to make the page feel like a flagship launch instead of safe centered product marketing.

## Best when
- the brief says flagship, launch, founder-led, anti-generic, or brand-forward
- product marketing needs confidence without experimental chaos
- the page should feel bold, directional, and conversion-aware
- motion tolerance is low to medium

## Distinguishing signals
- more structured and strategic than `neo-brutalism`
- more launch-driven than `editorial-minimalism`
- less atmospheric than `dark-glow-aurora`
- wins through hierarchy and placement more than effects

## Layout directives
- build an off-axis hero with one obvious focal move
- vary section compositions instead of repeating equal blocks
- let proof and CTA sections feel heavier than supporting content
- use asymmetry to create momentum, not confusion

## Typography and surface directives
- use direct, confident headlines with stronger scale jumps
- keep body copy functional and conversion-aware
- let surfaces add weight without turning the page into card spam
- prefer placement contrast before decorative contrast

## Component behavior
- CTA should feel timely and assertive
- proof blocks should look earned, not pasted in
- feature sections should change rhythm from one another
- nav should stay clear and relatively plain

## Avoid
- centered generic heroes
- repeated three-column feature grids
- decorative cards with no narrative role
- bold palette without stronger structure

## Review and conversion cues
- If the page feels hard-edged and graphic-first, it is drifting toward `neo-brutalism`.
- If spectacle or atmosphere outruns hierarchy, it is drifting toward immersive-premium.
- To convert from safe SaaS marketing, break symmetry first, then increase CTA and proof contrast.
