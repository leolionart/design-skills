# Family High Agency

Use when UI should feel decisive, brand-forward, and anti-template without collapsing into chaos.

## Handle these jobs
- design a flagship landing page direction
- sharpen weak, centered, generic heroes
- review whether hierarchy feels commanding enough
- convert safe product marketing into stronger launch energy

## Family thesis
Build momentum through asymmetry, decisive hierarchy, and section rhythm changes. Keep clarity, but avoid the safe centered-template feel.

## Use this family when
- the product needs launch energy
- the brand wants confidence without visual noise
- the page should feel bold but still structured

## Avoid this family when
- the brief is reading-first and quiet
- the interface is heavily operational or dashboard-dense
- the goal is soft friendliness rather than assertiveness

## Always cover
- hero asymmetry and focal priority
- section rhythm and pacing shifts
- confident CTA treatment
- proof blocks with weight, not card spam
- what generic patterns to remove

## Output contract
Return:
1. family thesis
2. layout language
3. typography and CTA treatment
4. component directives
5. avoid list
6. review or conversion moves

## Guardrails
- Keep one dominant focal move per section.
- Use asymmetry with purpose, not randomness.
- Do not fall back to equal-width feature grids unless the brief demands neutrality.
- Keep loudness below experimental-loud; this family is forceful, not chaotic.
