# Family Grid Product

Use when the interface must explain product value fast through modular structure, clear grouping, and practical conversion-oriented clarity.

## Handle these jobs
- design feature-heavy landing pages
- structure SaaS or AI explainers
- review whether modular blocks scan well
- convert atmospheric or quiet layouts into more practical product marketing

## Family thesis
Organize information into strong, readable modules. Keep density high enough to be useful and low enough to stay legible.

## Use this family when
- the brief mentions SaaS, AI, dashboard, feature overview, or fast scanning
- the page must balance product explanation and conversion
- clarity matters more than spectacle

## Avoid this family when
- the core value is brand statement over product explanation
- the page should feel poetic, cinematic, or deliberately disruptive
- the design needs strong softness or tactile material language

## Always cover
- module hierarchy
- tile sizing and grouping
- proof and CTA placement
- density control
- which repeated card patterns to simplify

## Output contract
Return:
1. family thesis
2. structure and grouping language
3. typography and proof treatment
4. component directives
5. avoid list
6. review or conversion moves

## Guardrails
- Do not make every card equal.
- Encode priority through scale, placement, and grouping.
- Keep trust signals and CTA connected to the narrative.
- Avoid decorative noise that weakens scanning.
