# Doctrine

## Thesis
Build premium atmosphere through depth, light, staging, and reveal. Let spectacle reinforce focus, not replace it.

## Visual grammar
- one focal scene
- layered surfaces or depth cues
- selective glow, blur, or object emphasis
- cinematic pacing where appropriate
- atmosphere with readable anchors

## Typography
- type supports the scene, then restores clarity
- headlines can be dramatic but must stay legible
- supporting text needs contrast discipline

## Components
- hero is usually the main stage
- cards and panels should feel layered, not random
- CTA should survive the visual richness

## Motion
Use supportive-to-narrative motion. Tie reveals to hierarchy, not novelty.

## Anti-patterns
- glow everywhere
- blur that weakens readability
- fake premium made only of gradients and shadow
- 3D with no structural role
- spectacle that buries CTA and proof
