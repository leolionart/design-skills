# Recipes

## Exemplar chooser
- `neo-brutalism` -> use for hard-edged, high-contrast, blocky product or campaign layouts with strong structure.
- `anti-design` -> use for controlled disorder, raw tension, and deliberate anti-template composition.
- `y2k-retro-futurism` -> use for glossy, nostalgic, millennium-coded tech/fashion energy.
- `cute-alism-kawaii-brutalism` -> use for playful, character-led, high-contrast interfaces that feel cheerful and punchy.
- `brutalist-editorial-zine` -> use for indie editorial, cutout collage, or publication-inspired cultural layouts.

## Quick differentiation rules
- Choose `brutalist-editorial-zine` over `anti-design` when the composition should feel like a publication or zine rather than general disruption.
- Choose `cute-alism-kawaii-brutalism` over `neo-brutalism` when the energy should feel friendly, mascot-like, or youth-oriented.
- Choose `y2k-retro-futurism` over `neo-brutalism` when glossy nostalgia and techno styling matter more than hard borders.
- Choose `anti-design` only when deliberate tension is the main thesis, not just loud color.

## Safe blends
- `neo-brutalism` + high-agency for stronger product clarity
- `brutalist-editorial-zine` + editorial-typography for cultural publishing
- `cute-alism-kawaii-brutalism` + tactile-organic for softer playful consumer work

## Escalation rule
Pick the disruptive move first: hard edge, raw disorder, glossy nostalgia, cute punchiness, or editorial collage. Then open only the matching exemplar pack to keep the style authored instead of noisy.
