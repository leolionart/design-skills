## NAAI style differentiation details (all active styles)
### `maximalist`

**NAAI profile**
- Name: Maximalist
- NAAI family: deconstructed-experimental
- Local family mapping: `experimental-loud`
- Availability: Live demo available.

**Core distinction**
Maximalist design favors visual abundance: layered content, color, texture, typography, and ornament appear together to create a bold, deliberate sense of richness rather than restraint or reduction.

**Page intent**
A visually dense direction built on layered graphics, rich ornament, and expressive abundance.

**Structural language**
Type, imagery, texture, and color stack in controlled layers to create high density while preserving rhythm.

**Supporting treatments**
- layered collage.
- rich ornament.
- dense high-contrast typography.

**Imagery mode**
Dense collage, alternating type scales, strong textures, repeating patterns, and richly contrasted palettes.

**Preview cues (NAAI)**
- Dense.
- Bold.
- Expressive.

**Avoid**
- Many layers without clear hierarchy.
- Color, texture, and type competing so aggressively that the focal point disappears.

**Keywords**
- maximalist.
- maximalism.
- layered collage.

**Differentiation guidance for AI**
- Keep this style anchored in its NAAI page intent and structural language before adjusting color.
- Use the supporting treatments together as a system, not as isolated decorative effects.
- Maintain the stated imagery mode and avoid list to prevent drift into nearby styles.
- Keep `maximalist` anchored in type, imagery, texture, and color stack in controlled layers to create high density while preserving rhythm; avoid drifting toward `dada-deconstructed` (deconstructed collage and intentional visual collision with layered cut-out imagery, rotated type, scanned paper, torn texture, and components arranged like a deliberately broken editorial spread).
- Keep `maximalist` anchored in type, imagery, texture, and color stack in controlled layers to create high density while preserving rhythm; avoid drifting toward `expressionist-graphics` (expressive graphic texture with brush strokes, scanned texture, emotional collage, and poster-like image treatment).

- Use preview cues and keywords as guardrails when expanding prompts or generating variants.
