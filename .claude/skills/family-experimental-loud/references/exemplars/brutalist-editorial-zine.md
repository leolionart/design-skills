# Brutalist Editorial Zine

## Thesis
Mix editorial composition and publication energy with raw, hard-edged tension so the interface feels like an authored cultural artifact rather than a standard website.

## Best when
- the brief says zine, editorial collage, publication-inspired, indie culture, fashion editorial, or poster-like website
- the project is content-led, campaign-led, or culture-led
- some readability tension is acceptable in exchange for strong identity
- the page can prioritize statement over conversion smoothness

## Distinguishing signals
- more publication-structured than `anti-design`
- more editorial and text-image layered than `neo-brutalism`
- less glossy and nostalgic than `y2k-retro-futurism`
- needs one typographic spine even when the layout gets rougher

## Layout directives
- use layered text-image compositions with one clear reading spine
- let sections feel like spreads, not app screens
- use overlap, interruption, and collage deliberately
- preserve at least one dominant flow through each section

## Typography and surface directives
- typography can feel raw, publication-like, or broken in rhythm
- type/image relationships matter more than polished surfaces
- borders and blocks can feel cutout or pasted, not refined
- imagery should support a cultural-editorial mood rather than clean product marketing

## Component behavior
- nav and CTA can look more like editorial artifacts than polished UI controls
- content modules can overlap or interrupt each other if hierarchy stays recoverable
- proof should feel cultural or contextual, not startup-social-proof generic

## Avoid
- random disorder with no reading path
- glossy retro cues that turn it into `y2k-retro-futurism`
- rawness so strong that all hierarchy disappears
- over-polishing that kills the zine feel

## Review and conversion cues
- If the page feels chaotic but not publication-like, it is drifting toward `anti-design`.
- If the page feels blocky and productish, it is drifting toward `neo-brutalism`.
- To convert from `anti-design`, restore an editorial spine and stronger text-image composition logic.
