# Y2K Retro Futurism

## Thesis
Use glossy gradients, chrome hints, millennium-tech nostalgia, and polished weirdness to create a page that feels playfully futuristic rather than purely brutal or raw.

## Best when
- the brief says Y2K, retro-futurism, digital nostalgia, glossy techno, chrome, or millennium-era future
- fashion, music, culture, campaign, or youth brands want nostalgia with polish
- the page should feel playful, glossy, and slightly sci-fi
- motion tolerance is medium

## Distinguishing signals
- glossier and more nostalgic than `neo-brutalism`
- less reflective-premium than `iridescent-holographic-chrome`
- less raw and disordered than `anti-design`
- less publication-like than `brutalist-editorial-zine`

## Layout directives
- keep the structure bold enough to hold nostalgic styling together
- use one or two glossy focal zones rather than nostalgia everywhere
- allow playful layer changes and techno accents without losing the reading path
- let sections feel campaign-like rather than purely product-safe

## Typography and surface directives
- use techno, glossy, or slightly stylized type with readable anchors
- let chrome and gradient accents support the theme without turning every surface metallic
- keep palette vivid but authored
- use shine, gloss, and nostalgia as mood rather than clutter

## Component behavior
- hero can carry most of the retro-futurist load
- CTA should survive the gloss and remain readable
- badges, chips, or accents can hold stronger nostalgic cues than body sections
- imagery can lean music-tech, fashion-tech, or digital-culture coded

## Avoid
- chrome on every surface
- nostalgia so heavy that the page becomes parody
- raw disorder that really belongs to `anti-design`
- premium reflective minimalism that should become `iridescent-holographic-chrome`

## Review and conversion cues
- If the page feels more premium-reflective than nostalgic, it is drifting toward `iridescent-holographic-chrome`.
- If the page feels mostly hard-edged and border-driven, it is drifting toward `neo-brutalism`.
- To convert from cleaner experimental work, add glossy material cues before breaking structure.
