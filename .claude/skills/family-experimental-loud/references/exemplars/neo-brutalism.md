# Neo Brutalism

## Thesis
Use thick borders, hard shadows, loud contrast, and blocky confidence to make the interface feel assertive, graphic, and unmistakably anti-clean.

## Best when
- the brief says neo-brutalism, hard-edged, bold border, hard shadow, anti-clean, or loud campaign layout
- startups, studios, brand pages, or creative launches need immediate visual force
- the page should feel direct and poster-like rather than polished
- motion tolerance is low to medium

## Distinguishing signals
- more structured and product-legible than `anti-design`
- harder and less friendly than `cute-alism-kawaii-brutalism`
- less glossy-nostalgic than `y2k-retro-futurism`
- more block-forward and assertive than `brutalist-editorial-zine`

## Layout directives
- use bold blocks with one obvious reading path
- let borders, scale, and placement create tension before adding many decorative accents
- keep sections graphic and punchy, not collage-heavy
- use strong anchors so the page feels authored instead of merely loud

## Typography and surface directives
- use blunt, confident type with strong size contrast
- keep borders visible and hard enough to define the style
- use hard shadow and flat color strategically, not on every surface equally
- avoid softening the page into a friendlier family by accident

## Component behavior
- CTA can feel forceful and immediate
- cards should feel like blocks with clear weight
- nav can be bolder than usual, but still readable
- proof and feature sections should keep structure even under visual aggression

## Avoid
- every element shouting at once
- raw disorder that loses hierarchy
- nostalgic gloss that should become `y2k-retro-futurism`
- friendliness that should become `cute-alism-kawaii-brutalism`

## Review and conversion cues
- If the page feels disrupted but not clearly structured, it is drifting toward `anti-design`.
- If it feels playful or mascot-led, it is drifting toward `cute-alism-kawaii-brutalism`.
- To convert from safer launch pages, increase border and shadow force after locking the main reading path.
