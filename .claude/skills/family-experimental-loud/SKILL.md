# Family Experimental Loud

Use when the interface should feel disruptive, culture-led, visually tense, and intentionally unlike safe product marketing defaults.

## Handle these jobs
- design anti-template campaigns or brand pages
- review bold or chaotic UI for actual control
- convert safe layouts into louder statements
- choose between neo-brutalist, Y2K, and anti-design expressions

## Family thesis
Create tension on purpose. Make boldness feel authored, not random.

## Use this family when
- the brief mentions disruptive, anti-template, loud, Y2K, brutalist, culture-led, or experimental
- the brand wants strong visual personality
- the page can trade some neutrality for memorability

## Avoid this family when
- the task is trust-heavy, enterprise, or operational
- the page must maximize calm readability
- the team wants premium restraint instead of disruption

## Always cover
- source of tension
- hierarchy under pressure
- type, border, and surface aggression
- what safe defaults to break
- what chaos must still be controlled

## Output contract
Return:
1. family thesis
2. tension and hierarchy language
3. typography and surface treatment
4. component directives
5. avoid list
6. review or conversion moves

## Guardrails
- Pick one dominant disruptive move first.
- Keep one readable hierarchy spine.
- Do not let every element scream equally.
- Explain the trade-off between memorability and usability.
